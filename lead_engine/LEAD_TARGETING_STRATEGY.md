# Lucid-Lab Lead Targeting Strategy

Last updated: 27 avril 2026

## Core Positioning

Lucid-Lab sells custom AI automation and operational systems for companies that have manual, repetitive, revenue-impacting workflows.

The strongest positioning is not generic "AI automation for everyone." The best wedge is helping operationally busy companies stop losing leads, time, and customer satisfaction because of slow follow-up, manual customer handling, poor CRM discipline, and scattered tools.

Lucid-Lab's strongest proof point is a long-term rental company project involving:
- Customer support automation
- Review management
- Marketing campaign support
- Sales escalation tools
- FAQ automation
- Future CRM build-out
- Progressive reduction of human actions related to acquiring and managing customers

This case study can be referenced as a long-term rental company, anonymized if needed.

## Commercial Model

### Custom Builds

Custom builds start at:

**€5k/month retainer**

This is the target pricing floor for serious custom AI and automation systems.

### Discovery Call

Lucid-Lab offers a free 30-minute discovery call to understand:
- The client's business
- Their current operational problems
- Their tools and workflow
- Whether there is a real automation opportunity
- Whether Lucid-Lab is a good fit

### Paid Audit

If the prospect is interested but does not know exactly what should be built, Lucid-Lab proposes a paid audit.

The audit:
- Starts at **€2k**
- Scales proportionally with company size and complexity
- Diagnoses operational bottlenecks
- Maps automation opportunities
- Defines a concrete build roadmap

Recommended audit name:

**Lead Leakage & Operations Audit**

Why this name works:
- It is concrete and painful
- It connects directly to lost revenue
- It avoids vague "AI strategy" language
- It gives the buyer a clear reason to act

Alternative names:
- AI Operations Audit
- Automation Diagnostic
- Revenue Operations Automation Audit
- Client Acquisition Systems Audit

## Primary Target Niche

### Best First Niche

**Mobility, vehicle rental, long-term rental, transport, tourism, and experience operators**

This is the best niche to scale into first because:
- Lucid-Lab already has relevant proof from a long-term rental company
- These businesses receive many inbound questions and booking requests
- They often rely on WhatsApp, email, forms, phone calls, and manual follow-up
- Missed response time directly means lost revenue
- Reviews and customer experience are highly important
- The same core system can be reused across similar companies

## Main Pain Signals

Prioritize companies showing one or more of these problems:

- Too many inbound messages
- Slow lead follow-up
- Manual booking or quoting
- Poor CRM discipline
- Leads falling through the cracks
- Weak customer support process
- Bad or unmanaged review workflows
- Manual marketing follow-up
- Repetitive customer questions
- Multiple tools that do not talk to each other
- Heavy reliance on WhatsApp, email, spreadsheets, or forms

## Recommended First-Step Offer

Best name:

**Lead Leakage & Operations Audit**

French version:

**Audit Fuites de Leads & Opérations**

Positioning:

A paid audit for companies that know they are losing time or revenue in manual customer handling, but do not yet know what system should be built.

The audit should identify:
- Where leads are lost
- Where response time is too slow
- Which customer questions can be automated
- Which workflows should remain human
- Which tools need to be connected
- What CRM structure is needed
- What automation should be built first
- Expected operational impact

## Sales Logic

The sales path should be:

1. Lead identified through LinkedIn, cold email, website research, or warm intro
2. Free 30-minute discovery call
3. If pain and budget are clear: propose custom build/retainer
4. If interest is real but scope is unclear: propose paid audit starting at €2k
5. Audit leads into roadmap
6. Roadmap leads into €5k+/month custom build retainer

## Outreach Qualification Questions

Use these questions during discovery:

1. How do new leads usually contact you today?
2. What happens after someone sends a request?
3. How fast do you usually respond?
4. Where do leads get lost or delayed?
5. Which questions do customers ask again and again?
6. Are bookings, quotes, or follow-ups handled manually?
7. What CRM or tracking system do you use today?
8. Who handles customer support and sales follow-up?
9. How many inbound requests do you get per week?
10. What happens when your team is busy, closed, or unavailable?
11. How do you manage reviews and post-sale follow-up?
12. What would be valuable to automate first if we could remove 20-40% of repetitive work?
